# How to Safely Rent Mid-Term on the Costa del Sol in 2025: Avoiding Scams and Ghost Properties

Deciding to spend your winter on the sunlit shores of Southern Spain should be a period of exciting anticipation, not a source of stress. For active expats seeking a milder climate and lower energy costs, the shift towards extended stays is becoming the new standard. However, navigating the Spanish real estate market from abroad can feel daunting, particularly when securing mid-term Winter Rentals Costa del Sol.

The reality is that the market is currently polarized. On one end, there are traditional real estate agencies focused solely on multi-year contracts or property sales. On the other, there are impersonal digital platforms like Airbnb or Booking.com, which often lack personalized support and, unfortunately, carry significant risks of scams.

If you are planning a Long Stay Spain for Retirees (typically between 1 to 9 months), understanding the new 2025 legal landscape and knowing how to protect your investment is absolutely critical.

---

## 1. The Danger: "Ghost Properties" and Fake Owners

The Spanish real estate market has, at times, suffered from a negative reputation regarding scams, particularly those involving "ghost properties" and "fake owners". These scams often target foreign clients who are understandably fearful and unable to view the property in person before transferring funds.

Fraudsters frequently utilize high-pressure tactics, demanding direct bank transfers to unknown accounts or untraceable wire services to secure a property that either doesn't exist or doesn't belong to them.

<HighlightBox type="warning">
  **Never** make payments through Western Union or direct transfers to unknown individuals. Always use an escrow account or a secure agency platform.
</HighlightBox>

---

## 2. The 2025 Regulatory Shift: What You Must Know

Adding to the complexity is a major regulatory shift. The new Decree from the Junta de Andalucía entering into force in 2025 is fundamentally reshaping the rental market.

<FeatureGrid>
  <div className="regulation-card">
    ### 🚫 Restrictions on Tourist Rentals (VFT)
    The new regulations introduce significant barriers to entry for short-term holiday rentals, with town halls like Málaga already implementing saturated zones where new licenses are denied.
  </div>
  <div className="regulation-card">
    ### ✅ The Seasonal Rental Solution (LAU)
    For stays of 1 to 9 months, the strategic opportunity lies within the Urban Leases Law (LAU) as a "Seasonal Lease". This type of contract is designed for non-habitual residence (such as wintering or health recovery) and does not require a tourist license.
  </div>
</FeatureGrid>

However, navigating this requires precise legal engineering in the contracts to justify the temporary nature of the stay and protect both the tenant and the owner, avoiding the legal insecurity of renting "under the table" or without a written agreement.

---

## 3. How to Protect Yourself When Booking from Abroad

To guarantee a secure and seamless transition to your winter home, you must treat your rental search with rigorous due diligence:

* **Demand Legal Verification:** Never rent a property without ensuring the person renting it to you has the legal right to do so. In Spain, this means verifying the property through the land registry (Nota Simple).
* **Look Beyond Summer Specs:** The coastal real estate market suffers from construction qualities designed strictly for summer. A property that is perfect in July can be uncomfortably cold in January without proper insulation and real heating systems.
