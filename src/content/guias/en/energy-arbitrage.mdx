## 1. The "Energy Tourism" Paradigm in 2026

The energy price crisis in Northern Europe has shifted from temporary to structural. While the UK and Germany struggle with high grid charges, Spain has successfully decoupled its energy mix thanks to massive renewable penetration, recording negative prices during peak daylight hours.

> **Quick Analysis:** Spain generates over 55% of its electricity from renewable sources. This allows residents to shift heavy consumption to hours of maximum solar radiation, reducing electricity bills to marginal levels.

## 2. HDD: The Scientific Metric of Savings

Arbitrage happens not just in price, but in demand. The **Heating Degree Days (HDD)** index measures how many degrees and for how long the outside temperature falls below the comfort threshold (15.5°C).

> A resident in Stockholm requires **90% more thermal energy** than one in Nerja. In London, annual thermal demand exceeds 1,270 HDD, while the Fuengirola-Nerja corridor stays below 300 HDD.
